# Positioning Clarity Check

This is not a surface-level "is your positioning clear" gate.

This is a **market research deep dive** that exists because understanding a business's customers better than they do is the only real leverage.

## Core Philosophy

If I understand your customers better than you do, I will always win.

And you will trust me — not because I impressed you with my knowledge of your business, but because I care about what you care about: your customers.

"I care about your customers" says more than "I care about your business."

## What This Does

1. **Analyze the product/offering** — what does the business actually sell, and is it solving a real problem?

2. **Competitive analysis** — who else is serving this audience, what gaps do they leave, and where is the whitespace?

3. **Customer deep-dive** — who are their customers really? What keeps them up at night? What have they tried before? What do they believe that isn't true?

4. **Gap identification** — what is the business missing about their own market? What opportunities are they blind to?

5. **Growth opportunity mapping** — connect the gaps to specific, concrete growth moves.

## How It Works

- Takes the business URL, offer description, and any available customer/audience context
- Does NOT trust the website alone — websites lie by omission
- Cross-references what the business says with what the market actually needs
- Returns a gap map showing: what the business thinks is true → what the market actually shows → the real opportunity

## Output

A positioning clarity brief with:
- **Market reality** — what the data and research say about this space
- **Customer truth** — what their customers actually need vs what the business thinks they need
- **Competitive whitespace** — gaps no one is filling
- **Growth opportunities** — 3-5 specific moves based on the gap analysis
- **Readiness score** — pass (proceed to audit) or blocked (fix positioning first)

## Boundaries

- Do not invent customer data; use observable signals from the website, reviews, competitors, and available research
- Do not skip this step because "we already know the business"
- If the business cannot articulate who their customer is, block the audit
- This is not a rewrite — it is a pre-audit research pass
- Route to `conversion-engine` only after positioning is sufficiently clear
