# Visibility Audit

Separate content-readiness diagnosis from technical measurement.

## Evidence Modes

### Pasted

Use pasted copy or screenshots to assess:

- entity and offer clarity
- answerability
- heading and content structure
- expertise and proof signals
- definitions, FAQs, and citable passages
- topical gaps visible in the supplied material

Mark technical checks `not measured`.

### Live URL

When tools are available, add evidence for:

- status codes and preferred URLs
- robots.txt and sitemap.xml
- titles, descriptions, headings, and links
- structured data
- accessibility
- mobile behavior and performance
- AI crawler policies

Do not claim rankings, backlinks, traffic, or AI citations without direct
evidence from an appropriate source.

## Workflow

1. State the evidence mode and tool access.
2. Record exact evidence for each finding.
3. Score only observed categories.
4. Separate critical discovery blockers from content-readiness improvements.
5. Prioritize by impact, confidence, and effort.
6. Return a seven-day plan for a quick audit or a two-to-four-week plan for a
   measured full audit.

## Categories

- technical discovery
- on-page clarity
- content quality and authority
- GEO readiness
- AEO readiness
- AI citability
- accessibility
- performance
- competitive gap, only when competitors are supplied

## Output

Return:

- evidence mode and tools used
- supported visibility score or `not measured`
- measured and unmeasured categories
- prioritized findings with exact evidence and fixes
- quick wins
- implementation plan
- missing evidence
- one next step

## Boundaries

- Do not rewrite the website; route rewrites to `conversion-engine`.
- Route combined conversion and visibility audits to `website-audit`.
- Do not treat content length, keyword repetition, or schema presence as proof
  of rankings or AI citations.
- Do not fabricate platform tests.
- The self-serve tool can be mentioned as in progress, but this skill is the publishable product today.
